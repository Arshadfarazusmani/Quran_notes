## ðŸ“– 1. Key Grammar Concept: Masdar (The Source)
* **Definition:** The root of a word/action (similar to the Infinitive or Verb in English).
* **Identification:**
    * **Urdu:** Ends in "na" (e.g., *Khana* (to eat), *Peena* (to drink), *Marna* (to hit)).
    * **Arabic:** Does not have a single fixed shape/marker like Urdu, but these roots form the basis of verbs.
* **Examples:**
    * *Zarb* (To hit)
    * *Sajdah* (To prostrate)
    * *Jamood* (To freeze/be static)

## ðŸ”„ 4. Active vs. Passive Voice (Ma'roof vs. Majhool)
How to change "He did" to "He was done to" (Passive Voice).

* **Active Voice (Past):** *Zaraba* (He hit).
* **Passive Voice Rule:**
    1.  First letter gets a **Pesh (Damma)**.
    2.  Second letter gets a **Zer (Kasra)**.
    3.  Third letter keeps the **Zabar (Fatha)**.
* **Pattern:** *Fu-'i-la*

### **Examples of Passive Voice**

| Active (He did...) | Passive (He was...) | Translation | Timestamp |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Zaraba** | **Zuriba** | **He was hit** | |
| **Qatala** | **Qutila** | **He was killed** | |

## ðŸ’¡ 5. Summary of Shapes
1.  **Zabar - Zabar - Zabar** (e.g., *Nasara*) = He did (Active).
2.  **Zabar - Zer - Zabar** (e.g., *Sami'a*) = He did (Active).
3.  **Zabar - Pesh - Zabar** (e.g., *Karuma*) = He became (Active).
4.  **Pesh - Zer - Zabar** (e.g., *Zuriba*) = He was done to (Passive).
